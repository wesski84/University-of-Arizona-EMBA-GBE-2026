Company Overview

BoCo Inc. is a Tokyo-based audio technology company specializing in bone conduction transduction—a technology that transmits sound through vibrations applied to the bones of the skull, bypassing the ear canal entirely. This fundamental technology shift enables new applications from hearing assistance for people with conductive hearing loss to general-purpose audio for consumers prioritizing situational awareness and ear health.

Founded in 2015, BoCo has positioned itself as a technology innovator in a nascent market, combining engineering excellence with social mission: designing products that improve quality of life for people with hearing differences while creating audio experiences accessible to all consumers.

Core Technology: Bone Conduction

How Bone Conduction Works

Traditional audio relies on sound waves traveling through air, entering the external ear canal, vibrating the eardrum, and transmitting through the middle ear ossicles to the inner ear (cochlea). Bone conduction bypasses this pathway, applying vibrations directly to the skull (typically at the temporal bone behind the ear), which transmit directly through bone to the inner ear.

Advantages for Hearing-Impaired Users

For people with conductive hearing loss (middle ear damage, chronic ear infections, ear canal malformations), bone conduction is a game-changer: it enables them to enjoy audio where traditional hearing aids or speakers fail. Additionally, bone conduction naturally leaves ear canals unobstructed, reducing infection risk and physical discomfort.

BoCo's Proprietary Innovations

BoCo has developed proprietary bone conduction transducer designs optimized for comfort, sound quality, and vibration efficiency. The company's technology is distinguished by compact form factor, long battery life, and multi-directional transduction enabling richer sound than earlier bone conduction devices.

Company History

Founding and Early Development (2015)

BoCo was founded in 2015 with a mission to create accessible, high-quality bone conduction audio. The founders recognized the vast hearing-impaired population globally and the limitations of existing hearing aids and audio solutions.

Crowdfunding Breakthrough (2017)

BoCo launched a successful Kickstarter campaign for its earsopen earphones, raising significant capital and validating market demand. Crowdfunding success provided both capital and early customer feedback, enabling product refinement.

Awards and Recognition (2018–2022)

The company earned prestigious design and innovation awards: Good Design Award 2021 and 2022 (Japan's premier design recognition), VGP Gold (audio equipment community award), and Sotokoto SDGs Award (sustainable development recognition). These validated both product quality and social mission.

Hearing Assistance and Social Mission (Ongoing)

BoCo has partnered with hearing health organizations and audiologists throughout Japan and internationally, positioning bone conduction products as legitimate hearing assistance devices. This contrasts with early bone conduction devices positioned as novelty consumer products.

Recent Developments (2024–2025)

The company continues expanding its product line and international presence while strengthening partnerships with hearing health communities. New product variants and integrations with hearing assistance services are in development.

Product Lines

earsopen Earphones

BoCo's primary consumer product—lightweight earphones designed for comfort during extended wear. The earsopen line includes multiple variants (water-resistant versions, color options, special editions) targeting both hearing-impaired users and general consumers valuing ear canal freedom and situational awareness.

docodemoSPEAKER Surface Speaker

A surface-mounted speaker variant transmitting sound via bone conduction through flat surfaces (desks, tables, walls). This enables innovative audio experiences for accessibility and ambient sound in shared spaces.

Market Context

Bone Conduction Market Growth

The global bone conduction audio market is nascent but rapidly growing, driven by increasing hearing loss prevalence, aging populations, consumer interest in open-ear audio, and awareness of hearing health dangers from in-ear earphones. Major tech companies (Apple, Google, Samsung) are investing in bone conduction research.

Dual Market Strategy

BoCo pursues a dual-market approach: (1) the medical/accessibility market (hearing-impaired users, audiologists, hospitals) where bone conduction provides genuine therapeutic benefit, and (2) the consumer market (athletes, professionals, health-conscious consumers) where bone conduction offers lifestyle advantages. This diversification reduces dependence on hearing aid sales alone.

International Expansion

BoCo is expanding internationally, entering markets across Europe, North America, and Asia. Each market has distinct regulatory requirements (FDA for medical devices in US, CE marking in EU, PMDA in Japan) and reimbursement structures affecting market entry strategy.
